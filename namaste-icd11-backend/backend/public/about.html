About the Platform

Understanding NAMASTE to ICD-11 terminology mapping

The Problem Statement

Traditional medicine systems like Ayurveda have rich terminology for diseases, conditions, and treatments that are not directly aligned with modern international medical classifications such as ICD-11. This creates challenges in:

  • Electronic Health Record (EHR) integration
  • Healthcare data analytics and reporting
  • Research and evidence generation
  • Reimbursement and insurance claims
  • Interoperability between traditional and modern healthcare systems
  • Regulatory compliance and documentation

The NAMASTE (National AYUSH Morbidity and Standardized Terminologies) initiative by the Ministry of AYUSH, Government of India, aims to standardize Ayurveda terminology and bridge this gap by providing standardized codes that can be mapped to international classifications like ICD-11.

Why Mapping Is Needed

Clinical Integration

Enables integration of traditional medicine practices with modern healthcare systems, allowing practitioners to document treatments using both NAMASTE and ICD-11 codes.

Data Analytics

Facilitates evidence-based research by enabling statistical analysis of traditional medicine outcomes using standardized international classification systems.

Global Recognition

Promotes global recognition and acceptance of traditional medicine systems by aligning with internationally recognized medical terminologies.

Regulatory Compliance

Ensures compliance with healthcare regulations and standards that require the use of internationally recognized medical codes for documentation.

Standards Referenced

NAMASTE

National AYUSH Morbidity and Standardized Terminologies

  • Standardized terminology for Ayurveda, Yoga, Unani, Siddha, and Homeopathy
  • Over 50,000 standardized terms for diseases, symptoms, and treatments
  • Developed by the Ministry of AYUSH, Government of India
  • Enables systematic documentation of traditional medicine practices

ICD-11

International Classification of Diseases, 11th Revision

  • World Health Organization's medical classification system
  • Used globally for mortality and morbidity statistics
  • Includes traditional medicine chapters
  • Enables international health data comparison

FHIR R4

Fast Healthcare Interoperability Resources

  • Modern healthcare data exchange standard
  • RESTful API design for healthcare interoperability
  • Supports various healthcare data types including terminology
  • Enables integration with Electronic Health Records

SNOMED CT

Systematized Nomenclature of Medicine - Clinical Terms

  • Comprehensive clinical terminology
  • Used for encoding clinical health information
  • Supports clinical decision support systems
  • Enables semantic interoperability
